Research Direction

Applied AI for infrastructure intelligence and scalable generative systems

Current PhD-Focused Research Statement

My PhD research centers on applied artificial intelligence and machine learning, leveraging complex infrastructure and construction environments as practical domains for testing advanced computational models. I am currently conducting a systematic literature review on the application and integration of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and video models for advanced analytics.

By exploring foundational spatiotemporal data analysis, I use construction management technology as a testing ground to build scalable generative AI solutions. This interdisciplinary approach strengthens my technical base for a long-term goal: contributing as a professor in applied computer science.
