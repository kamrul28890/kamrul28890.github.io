Research

Applied AI for Construction Intelligence

My research applies Vision-Language Models and video AI to automate safety detection, progress tracking, and decision-making on construction sites - bridging the gap between frontier AI and real-world infrastructure.

Statement

Construction sites are among the most information-rich, yet least digitized environments in existence. Workers, machines, materials, and hazards interact in highly dynamic ways - yet most site management still relies on manual observation, paper-based reporting, and subjective judgment.

My PhD research, based at the Wii Lab (Worker-centric Intelligent Infrastructure) under Dr. Heung Jin Oh at Purdue University, asks: what happens when we give construction sites the ability to see, understand, and reason?

I work specifically on Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and video foundation models - systems that can jointly process visual inputs (images, video streams) and generate semantic understanding in natural language. The applications range from automated PPE detection and unsafe behavior flagging, to temporal progress tracking across construction phases, to intelligent documentation generation.

My background in civil/geotechnical engineering, combined with graduate training in big data analytics and deep learning, gives me an unusual vantage point: I understand both the computational machinery of modern AI and the practical constraints of the construction industry it aims to serve.

Focus Areas
01

Vision-Language Models in Construction

VLMs like GPT-4V, LLaVA, and emerging open-source models can process construction site images and video frames while answering natural language queries about what they observe. My work investigates how to fine-tune, prompt, and deploy these models for:

  • Real-time PPE and safety compliance detection
  • Worker activity recognition and task classification
  • Hazard identification and automatic reporting
  • Equipment and material status monitoring

02

Video Intelligence & Progress Tracking

Construction projects unfold over time - video models that understand spatiotemporal dynamics can automate the tedious task of progress documentation, comparing real-time site footage against BIM models and project schedules.

Lab

Wii Lab

Worker-centric Intelligent Infrastructure (S2-WIDE)

Directed by Dr. Heung Jin Oh, Wii Lab advances Smart and Sustainable Worker-centric Intelligent infrastructure for Data-driven Evolution in construction. The lab uses large-scale data, spatial computing, AI, and robotics to understand how workers, skills, and intelligent tools interact over time and space - building practical models that support industry, educators, and public agencies.
